A New Way to Play

Welcome, brave adventurers, to Elemental Forge! This card game combines strategic card play, deck building, and a touch of fate to create a unique experience. Your goal is to be the first to collect 15 points by crafting powerful elemental combinations.

Game Setup

  • A standard 52-card deck with Jokers.
  • A set of four dice.
  • Two to four players.

Card Values:

  • Aces = 1 point
  • 2-10 = Face value
  • Jacks, Queens, Kings = 10 points
  • Jokers (Catalyst Cards): Doubles the value of the combined card. Does not have a special effect.

Game Play

1. The Starting Hand and "The Forge"

Each player starts with a hand of 5 cards. The remaining cards form the Draw Deck. The top card of the Draw Deck is flipped over and becomes the first card in the Forge Pile, where players will play their cards.

2. Your Turn

On your turn, you must choose one of the following actions:

  • Draw: Take the top two cards from the Draw Deck.
  • Play and Combine: Play a card from your hand onto the Forge Pile to create a new "elemental" card combination.
  • Draw and Discard: Discard as many cards as you want from your hand and draw the same number of cards from the Draw Deck.

Elemental Combinations & Effects

When you play a card from your hand onto the Forge Pile, it combines with the top card on that pile. The new card's value is the sum of the two cards. You gain the points and a special effect based on the cards' color or suit. The new card is placed at the bottom of the Draw Deck.

Elemental Effects:

  • Fire Element (Red Cards): Combining two red cards creates Ignite. You may then immediately discard one card from your hand and force another player to also discard one card.
  • Water Element (Black Cards): Combining two black cards creates Hydroblast. You can choose to draw one extra card from the deck and add it to your hand.
  • Air Element (Spades & Clubs): Combining two cards of the same suit creates Gust. You may then swap one card from your hand with a card in another player's hand.
  • Earth Element (Diamonds & Hearts): Combining two cards of the same suit creates Quake. You can then force all players to discard one card from their hand.

Dice Roll Attacks

After you create an elemental combination, you can choose to "attack" another player.

  • Declare which player you are attacking.
  • Roll one die for every 5 points in your combination. For example, a 12-point combo means you roll two dice.
  • Each time a die lands on a 6, it is a Hit.
  • The attacked player can block hits by discarding a card from their hand. For each card they discard, they roll one die. If the die lands on a 4, 5, or 6, they have successfully blocked a hit.
  • For each unblocked hit, the attacked player loses that many points from their score.

How to Win

The first player to reach 15 points wins the game!
